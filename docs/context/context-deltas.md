# Oxygen Shuttle — Feedstock Deltas from Lunar Mare Baseline

All entries describe deviations from the lunar mare six-campaign baseline.
Only changed parameters are listed; unlisted parameters = same as baseline.

---

## Highland (Appendix F) — Al-Rich, Fe-Poor Lunar

**Yield shifts:**
- Fe: ~15–25 kg/t (vs 85–130 mare) — 3–4× less
- Ti: ~2–5 kg/t (vs 12–15) — nearly absent
- Al: ~87–99 kg/t (vs 65–80) — premium Al feedstock
- Mg: ~11–27 kg/t from C4 pyrolysis
- Si: comparable to mare from MRE
- O₂: ~370–440 kg/t

**Campaign changes:**
- C1: less Na/K yield; less early Fe
- C2: lower Fe yield; both paths function normally
- C3: abbreviated (less Fe/Ti to reduce)
- C4: lower Mg yield
- C5: MRE operates on more Al₂O₃-rich melt
- C6: Mg stoichiometric draw 100–115 kg (vs 50–60 mare); inventory ~neutral at ≥80% recovery

**Energy:** Branch Two ~1100–1800 kWh/t electrical (70–75% savings vs all-MRE)

**Ceramics advantage:** mullite (natural 70:30 Al₂O₃:SiO₂ after extraction), anorthite (free — highland IS anorthite), CA cement (natural 50:50 CaO:Al₂O₃), near-pure alumina (>80% Al₂O₃ after full extraction)

**Ceramics disadvantage:** less cordierite (low MgO), no perovskite (no TiO₂)

## S-Type Asteroid Silicate (Appendix G) — Mg-Dominant, Al/Ca-Poor

**Defining feature:** 30–38% MgO; olivine-pyroxene mineralogy; not a CMAS glass.

**Pre-processing:** Magnetic separation yields 100–200 kg Fe-Ni/t of bulk rock at zero energy. FeS → 25–30 kg S at 600–900°C (sulfur trap needed). +100°C gap between S and Na/K evolution.

**Campaign changes:**
- C0: **Enhanced** — magnetic sep + sulfur recovery. Optional O₂ conditioning blow for uncertain feedstock (oldhamite CaS)
- C1: 2–3× Na/K stock (6–9 kg Na vs ~3 lunar). Early Fe modest.
- C2: Variable Fe (H-chondrite 30–40 kg, LL 55–70 kg). **Higher liquidus** (1350–1450°C vs 1150–1250); 15–25% more melting energy.
- C3: **Nearly vestigial** — no Ti target (0.1–0.2% TiO₂). Minor Fe polish only.
- C4: **DOMINANT campaign.** 100–200 kg Mg/t (vs 35–65 lunar). a(MgO) approaches unity in olivine field.
- C5: Normal MRE on SiO₂-rich residual (50–60% SiO₂ after Mg removal).
- C6: **Vestigial.** 10–20 kg Al (2–4% Al₂O₃). Surplus Mg >> stoichiometric need.

**Energy:** ~900–1600 kWh/t electrical. Solar thermal higher due to melting energy.

**Ceramics:** Reduced to forsterite (Mg₂SiO₄) + enstatite (MgSiO₃) only. No spinel/mullite/anorthite/CA cement (insufficient Al₂O₃/CaO).

**Gravity:** micro-g requires redesign for: metal tapping (centrifugal/EM pump), Mg injection (aggressive induction stirring), condensation (wicking surfaces). Vapour-phase processes unaffected.

**Assessment:** Architecture applies but shuttle elegance underutilised. Degrades to Fe(magnetic) + Mg(pyrolysis) + Si(MRE) refinery.

## Bulk M-Type Asteroid (Appendix H) — Iron Mine with Silicate Byproduct

**Process:** Simplified to melt → density separation (7.9 vs 2.8 g/cm³) → tap metal → process residual silicate per S-type flowchart.

**Metal phase:** 300–500 kg/t Fe-Ni-Co alloy (88–92% Fe, 5–10% Ni, 0.3–0.5% Co). Natural Invar/Kovar analog. Requires only melting + tapping.

**Silicate phase:** ~500 kg/t. Same as M-type silicate (see S-type deltas, extreme end).
- C2–3: vestigial (2–5% FeO → 10–25 kg Fe)
- C4: dominant (60–110 kg Mg)
- C5: 50–90 kg Si
- C6: minimal (3–8 kg Al)

**pO₂ self-buffering:** Large Fe pool buffers pO₂ ~1e-9 bar via Fe/FeO equilibrium. SiO suppressed without external control.

**Sulfur:** Partitions into metal phase. Tapped alloy 0.5–2% S. Management: oxidative ladle refining (CO₂ blow) or lime injection (CaO + S → CaS).

**Energy:** ~400–900 kWh/t electrical. Most mass is metallic Fe needing only melting.

**Confidence:** LOWEST of any feedstock. Psyche mission (Aug 2029) will constrain.

## Mars Basalt (Appendix I) — CO₂ Atmosphere + Sulfates + High Alkali

**Environment constraint:** ~6 mbar surface pressure → no deep vacuum.
**Environment advantages:** Free CO₂ carrier gas; CO₂ thermal dissociation → natural pO₂ source; 5–10× Na/K stock; sulfur/chlorine feedstocks unlock REE extraction.

**Campaign changes:**
- C0: **Extended** with carbon pre-reduction:
  - Phase 0a (<400°C): perchlorate decomposition → O₂ + Cl⁻
  - Phase 0b (600–1050°C): C rod injection reduces sulfates (FeSO₄ @600–700, MgSO₄ @700–900, CaSO₄ @900–1050°C). Carbon: ~20–40 kg/t (manufactured from Mars CO₂ via electrolysis + Boudouard).
  - Phase 0c (1050–1200°C): final sulfate/halide cleanup. C self-gasifies via Boudouard.
- C1+C2: **Merged** into single continuous ramp under flowing CO₂. CO₂ dissociation provides pO₂ naturally.
- CO₂ is NOT inert: Fe(l) + CO₂(g) → FeO(melt) + CO(g) competes at millibar pCO₂.
- C3–C6: functionally same as lunar; larger Na/K inventory accelerates shuttle.

**Condensation train additions:** halide salt trap (650–900°C, ~6–10 kg NaCl/KCl), SO₂ sorbent (400–500°C, Fe granule: Fe + SO₂ → FeS + O₂), HCl/HF scrubber (200–350°C, Na₂CO₃ packed bed), CO₂/CO gas separator.

**Critical safety:** SO₂ must NEVER contact metallic Na (violently reactive: 2Na + SO₂ → Na₂O + S).

**Altitude-dependent:** Low sites (Hellas 11 mbar): better SiO suppression, higher pump cost. High sites (Tharsis 2.5 mbar): easier pumpdown, lower suppression. Sweet spot: 3–5 km elevation (3–4 mbar).

**Bonus products:** NaCl/KCl salts (REE chemistry), sulfuric acid feedstock, CO propellant.

**Energy:** ~850–1500 kWh/t electrical + ~1450–2100 kWh/t solar thermal. Carbon production costs ~50–100 kWh.

## CI Carbonaceous Chondrite (Appendix J) — Volatile-Rich, Self-Cleaning

**Defining feature:** 10–20 wt% structural H₂O; 2–5% C; 2–6% S. ~200–350 kg/t lost as volatiles. The material is self-cleaning — indigenous carbon (30–50 kg) exceeds all reduction demand (10–27 kg).

**Extended Campaign 0 (5–10 hours):**
1. Organic pyrolysis (200–500°C) → hydrocarbons, CO₂; char remains
2. Phyllosilicate dehydroxylation (400–700°C) → H₂O; serpentine → forsterite + enstatite
3. Sulfide decomposition (500–900°C) → S₂ (no reductant needed — sulfides, not sulfates)
4. Carbonate decomposition (600–800°C) → CO₂
5. Magnetite reduction by CO (800–950°C) → wüstite
6. **Conditioning O₂ blow (1000–1050°C):** desulfurisation (S²⁻ + O₂ → SO₂), decarburisation (C + O₂ → CO₂), re-oxidation of incidental metallic Fe back to FeO. Consumes ~2–5 kg O₂. End criterion: SO₂/CO signals → baseline on IR spectrometer (~30–60 min).

**Post-conditioning:** clean anhydrous silicate, 650–800 kg/t. 25–35% FeO (all as FeO), 22–26% MgO, 32–40% SiO₂. Standard C1–C6 applies.

**Campaign changes (from lunar baseline):**
- C1: 4–6 kg Na (adequate shuttle stock)
- C2: **Highly productive** — 75–120 kg Fe/t (comparable to or exceeding lunar mare due to high FeO). Product is 3–8% Ni alloy (vapour-purified, zero S/C). Purity advantage over magnetically separated metal.
- C3: minor (Ti ~negligible at 0.08–0.12% TiO₂)
- C4: 55–85 kg Mg/t
- C5–C6: similar to baseline; 7–15 kg Al

**Volatile products (uniquely abundant):**
- H₂O: 100–170 kg/t (the standout product; 100–1000× more than any other feedstock)
- S: 25–40 kg
- Hydrocarbons: 15–30 kg
- CO₂/CO: 20–50 kg

**Energy:** ~950–1700 kWh/t electrical. Extended C0 degassing consumes ~400–700 kWh solar thermal.

**Surface dehydration caveat:** Ryugu samples showed ~50% less H₂O than pristine CI meteorites. Use full CI range as conservative upper bound, Ryugu values as realistic surface estimate.

## Ceres Regolith & Comet Nuclei (Appendix K) — Volatile-Extreme CI Extensions

**Defining feature:** Same anhydrous silicate after degassing as CI; only C0 changes.

**Campaign changes (both):**
- C0: massively extended/staged with extra cold-traps (NH₃, CO/CH₄, HCl).
- C1–C6: identical to CI (Mg-dominant, immediate surplus).
- Energy: +400–1000 kWh solar-thermal for C0 only; electrical unchanged (~950–1700 kWh/t).

**Ceres specifics:** 150–300 kg/t H₂O/ice + NH₃ salts → water + fertilizer bonus.
**Comet specifics:** 200–400 kg/t H₂O + CO/CH₄/NH₃ → propellant factory.
**Edge cases:** Ceres equatorial drier (use lower end); comet mantles dehydrated (use CI surface note); both benefit from existing induction stirring and IR endpoint control.
